Pyramids

Pyramids are large, ancient structures with triangular outer surfaces that converge to a point at the top. The most famous are the Egyptian pyramids, which were primarily built as tombs for pharaohs and their consorts during the Old and Middle Kingdom periods. The Great Pyramid of Giza, built for Pharaoh Khufu, is the largest of these, and it is the last remaining of the original Seven Wonders of the Ancient World.
